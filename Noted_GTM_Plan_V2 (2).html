The frameworks behind this plan

  • Sean Ellis PMF Survey validates the 100. The test: would 40%+ be very disappointed without Noted?
  • Eugene Schwartz's 5 Stages of Awareness sequences the content. Problem-aware content ships before product content.
  • April Dunford's Positioning anchors every message. Noted is the decision layer. Never the scheduling tool.
  • Van Westendorp + Charm Pricing shapes the price points. Details in the Pricing tab.
The core insight
Every LinkedIn tool fights over the content layer. Nobody has claimed the decision layer.

Taplio schedules. Planable approves. ClickUp tracks tasks. Not one remembers what the client decided three weeks ago. That gap is the entire business.

The primary buyer, in full

Who they are

The Overloaded Agency Owner

  • Founder-operator, team of 0 to 4
  • Runs 3 to 15 LinkedIn client accounts
  • Makes $3k to $25k monthly from retainers
  • 1 to 5 years in. Past hustle, hitting a ceiling.
  • US, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, UAE
How they think

Systems believer, tool buyer

  • Already spends $100 to $400 monthly on tools
  • Uses Notion or ClickUp, Slack or WhatsApp, Canva
  • Reads Hormozi, Nicolas Cole, Justin Welsh
  • Knows they are the bottleneck. Says it out loud.
  • Uses AI for drafts. Wants an ops layer, not a writer.

A day in their life

Monday, 9am. Three WhatsApp threads, two emails, one call recording. All contain client feedback from last week. None of it lives anywhere. They spend 30 minutes reconstructing decisions from memory. Then they brief the writer from that shaky memory. By Thursday, the client asks why the tone changed. It did not change. The decision got lost.

That Monday is the product's reason to exist. Every message we write points at it.

Step 1 · Build the list from Malik's profile

All recruiting happens from Malik Muzamil's personal profile. People buy early products from founders, not brands. The scorecard in Tab 02 filters every name.

Where the 100 come from

  1. Comment sections of competitor contentPeople commenting on Taplio and LinkedGood posts have the exact problem. Mine daily.
  2. Pain-signal searchSearch LinkedIn for phrases like "client feedback WhatsApp" and "agency onboarding chaos".
  3. Agency communitiesSlack groups, LinkedIn creator communities, freelance collectives. Give value for two weeks first.
  4. Second-degree referralsEvery yes gets asked: "Who else runs an agency like yours?"

Step 2 · The two-week pitch sprint

Once the list exists, pitch all 100 within 14 days. Momentum matters. A slow drip kills urgency.

Days 1–3
Warm-up, no pitchEngage with 3 posts per prospect. Meaningful comments only. Get recognized before appearing in their inbox.
Days 4–10
Personal pitch wave · 15 per dayReference their specific pain post. One question, not a pitch: "What happens to client feedback between Monday's call and next week?"
Days 11–14
Follow-up and closeOne follow-up per non-responder. Offer the spot plus founding pricing locked forever. Close the list publicly at 100.
The pitch never sells the tool. It sells the spot: early access, founding pricing locked forever, and a direct line to the team building for them.

Why we dropped the old pricing

$29/$79/$149 priced Noted like a proven suite. Noted is not proven yet. A pre-launch product cannot carry suite pricing against Kontentino's decade of reviews. The new pricing removes the decision friction entirely. It makes trying Noted cheaper than staying disorganized.

The psychology behind every number

  1. Left-digit effect: $17 reads as "teens"The brain anchors on the first digit. $17 feels a category cheaper than $20. It also undercuts Supergrow ($19) and AuthoredUp ($19.95) while doing more.
  2. The $50 barrier: $47 stays under it$50 is a mental approval threshold for solo founders. $47 clears it without a second thought. It is also below Taplio's real entry of $65.
  3. Charm endings in 7 signal founder-ledPrices ending in 7 read as direct and deliberate, not corporate. Consistent endings across tiers build pricing trust.
  4. Studio as the anchor, Agency as the choice$97 exists partly to make $47 feel safe and obvious. Classic center-stage effect. Most buyers pick the highlighted middle.
  5. Clean upgrade mathSolo to Agency is under 3x for 5x the clients. Agency to Studio is roughly 2x for unlimited. Every jump feels like a deal.
  6. Value framing: cheaper than one lost clientAverage retainer is $1,000+ monthly. Studio at $97 costs under 10% of one saved client. The ROI story writes itself.
  7. Founding lock creates urgency without discountingFounding pricing is a permanent lock, never a sale. It rewards belief, not hesitation. It dies publicly at spot 100.

Studio $97 vs suites $140–245

Flat where they multiply

Planable at 8 clients costs roughly $264 monthly. Kontentino gates approvals at $95+. Studio: $97, everything included.

Pricing rules, carried forward

  • Never discount publicly. Founding pricing is a lock, not a sale. Extended trials over discounts.
  • Trial mirrors the value cycle: one full client week, minimum 7 days.
  • Founding pricing dies permanently at spot 100. Announce the countdown at 80.
  • Validate willingness-to-pay in the feedback calls using Van Westendorp's four questions.
  • Revisit tiers only after 6 months of retention data. Never mid-quarter.
The Van Westendorp questions for the calls: At what price is Noted too expensive? Expensive but worth considering? A bargain? So cheap you would doubt the quality? Thirty answers will confirm or correct these numbers before launch.
